WILDLIFE SANCTUARY


Wildlife sanctuaries in India are blessed with rich biodiversity, varying topography, and exotic flora and fauna. Ecotourism hotspots like Kaziranga National Park, Kanha Tiger Reserve, and Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary set amid beautiful landscapes let visitors get a true taste of nature and adventure! Courtesy of the expansive forest cover and rich foliage in every corner of the country, there are plenty of animal reserves in India dedicated to protecting our natural resources as well as wildlife and birds, especially rare and endangered species. According to National Wildlife Database India, there are currently 573 wildlife sanctuaries, tiger reserves, and bird sanctuaries in India covering about 4% of its total land area!
